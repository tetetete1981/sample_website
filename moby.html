Moby-Dick : A classic tale of the sea

Moby-Dick by Hermann Melville begins with thes immortal words

Call me ismael. Somes years ago-never mind hoz long precisly-havinf little or no money in my pruse, and nothing particular to interest me on shore, i thought i would sail about a little and see the watery part of the world. It is a way i have of driving off the spleen and regulating the circulation.

After driving off his spleen (which can't be good for you), Ishmael then goes on in much the same vein for approximately one jillion pages. the only thing bigger the Moby Dick (who-- spoiler alert !-- is a giant white whale) is the book itself.
